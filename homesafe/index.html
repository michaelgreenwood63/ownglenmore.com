What Is It?

The problem it solves

Most sellers face the same two bad choices: sell first and scramble to find a new home under pressure, or buy first and carry two mortgages while waiting for your old home to sell. The Homesafe Program is a third option — one that eliminates both risks entirely.

Mark Jontz & Associates created this program after watching hundreds of clients get squeezed by the timing problem. It uses a specific contract structure — a clause most buyers and sellers don't even know exists — to let both transactions run in parallel and close on the same day.

We've done this 1,800+ times in Kelowna.

How It Works

Three steps. One move.

1
List with the right clause

Your home goes on the market with a "Subject to Finding Another Suitable Property" clause built into the listing. This gives you 30, 60, or 90 days after accepting an offer to find and secure your next home — before you're legally committed to selling. If you don't find something you love, you don't have to sell. Zero obligation.

2
Lock in your numbers

Once a buyer's conditional offer is accepted on your home, your selling price and closing date are fixed. You now know exactly how much you're netting and exactly when you need to be out. That certainty is what lets you shop for your next property without guessing — you know your budget to the dollar.

3
Both closings on the same day

We coordinate both transactions so they close simultaneously. On moving day, you hand over the keys to your old home — and receive the keys to your new one. One move. No rental overlap. No storage unit. No sleeping at your sister's place for six weeks while you wait.

Common Questions

What people ask us

Does the clause reduce what buyers are willing to offer?

Rarely, and not significantly. Buyers who want your specific home will accept the clause. In a market where move-up and move-down transactions are common, it's a well-understood condition that doesn't kill deals.

What if I don't find a home I like in the timeframe?

That's exactly what the "No Obligation to Sell" guarantee covers. If you don't find a property you love within your agreed window, the sale of your existing home doesn't proceed. You're protected.

Does this work if I'm buying in a different city or province?

It works within BC. Cross-province purchases introduce different timelines and contract structures — ask us and we'll tell you what's possible for your specific situation.

Is this different from just making conditional offers?

Yes. A conditional offer on the purchase side still leaves you exposed if your current home doesn't sell in time. The Homesafe Program protects both sides — the listing clause on your sale creates the window, and we coordinate the purchase timeline to close within it.
