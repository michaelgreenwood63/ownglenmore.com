Glenmore · Neighbourhood Guide

Amenities & Lifestyle

From world-class golf and Knox Mountain trails to Glenmore Landing shopping, named medical clinics, and a $251M recreation centre rebuild — Glenmore is one of the most self-contained neighbourhoods in Kelowna.

Grocery & Daily Needs
Two shopping hubs, everything covered

Glenmore's grocery options are anchored by two main commercial hubs — Glenmore Landing and Glenpark Village — each within minutes of most Glenmore addresses.

IGA — Glenmore Landing
Full Service Grocery

Full-service grocery at Glenmore's main commercial hub — Glenmore Landing at Harvey Ave and Glenmore Road. Butcher counter, bakery, produce, deli, and pharmacy. A neighbourhood anchor.

Save-On-Foods — Glenpark Village
Full Service Grocery

Premium grocery at the Glenpark Village commercial node. In-store pharmacy, bakery, deli, and full produce section. One of the better-stocked stores serving the Glenmore corridor.

Shoppers Drug Mart
Pharmacy + Convenience

Full pharmacy, convenience grocery, beauty, and health products at Glenpark Village. Convenient for prescriptions and top-ups between bigger shops.

Pharmasave Glenmore
Independent Pharmacy

Local independent pharmacy with personalized service. A solid alternative to chain pharmacies for prescriptions, OTC medications, and health products.

Dyck's IDA Pharmacy
Pharmacy

An established Kelowna institution — independent pharmacy offering prescription services, compounding, and personalized health consultations for the Glenmore community.

Orchard Park Area · 10 min
Major Retail · Nearby

Costco, Superstore, Walmart, and T&T Supermarket are a 10–15 minute drive via Harvey Avenue. All major grocery chains are well within reach for a weekly run.

🛍️
Shopping & Retail
Glenmore Landing to Glenpark Village

Two main commercial hubs serve Glenmore's daily retail needs — Glenmore Landing along Harvey Avenue and Glenpark Village near Kane Road. Orchard Park Mall is 10 minutes away.

Glenmore Landing
Main Shopping Hub

Glenmore's primary commercial centre at Harvey Ave and Glenmore Road — anchored by IGA with banks, restaurants, services, and retail surrounding it. The most walkable commercial node for residents in the southern part of the neighbourhood.

Glenpark Village
Commercial Node

Shopping hub near Kane Road serving North Glenmore — Save-On-Foods, Shoppers Drug Mart, restaurants, personal services, and retail. Increasingly the go-to for residents in the growing northern part of the neighbourhood.

Banks & Credit Unions
Financial Services

Multiple major banks and Interior Savings branches serve Glenmore. TD, RBC, and others accessible via Glenmore Landing and surrounding commercial areas.

Restaurants & Cafés
Dining

A growing dining scene within Glenmore — from local independent cafés to established chains. The Wilden community's planned Wilden Market Square will add more ground-floor dining and retail as North Glenmore continues to develop.

Home Hardware
Hardware & Home

Hardware, tools, paint, and home improvement supplies accessible from Glenmore. No drive across town needed for your weekend project.

Orchard Park Mall
Shopping Mall · 10 min

Kelowna's main shopping mall is 10 minutes from Glenmore via Harvey Avenue — anchored by major department and sports retailers with 150+ stores, a food court, and a cinema.

Parks & Trails
Knox Mountain to the Rail Trail

Glenmore sits at the base of Knox Mountain — Kelowna's largest park — with Dilworth Mountain, Brandt's Creek, Blair Pond, and the Okanagan Rail Trail all within the neighbourhood or a short drive away.

Knox Mountain Park
Regional Park · 502 Hectares

Kelowna's largest park sits at Glenmore's doorstep — 502 hectares of trails, viewpoints, and natural landscape. Popular for hiking, mountain biking, trail running, and the Apex viewpoint overlooking Okanagan Lake. One of the city's most used outdoor destinations.

Dilworth Mountain Park
Hiking · Views

Natural hillside park with trails, rocky outcrops, and sweeping views of the Glenmore valley and Okanagan Lake. Popular with hikers and dog walkers from the neighbourhood.

Brandt's Creek Linear Park
Linear Trail · Within Glenmore

A green corridor running through the heart of Glenmore — paved walking and cycling paths following Brandt's Creek. Connects residential streets to commercial areas and provides a traffic-free commuting and recreation route.

Blair Pond Park
Natural Area · Wetland

A natural wetland park and wildlife habitat within Glenmore — peaceful walking paths around a small pond, birdwatching, and natural landscape preservation in an increasingly built-up area.

Glenmore Recreation Park
Community Park · Sports Fields

Active community park in Glenmore with sports fields, playgrounds, and open green space. Hosts youth sports and community events. Upcoming park upgrades are included in the city's capital plan.

Okanagan Rail Trail
Multi-Use Trail · Regional

A 56-km paved multi-use trail following the former CN Rail corridor from Kelowna north through Lake Country to Vernon. One of BC's premier urban cycling and walking trails — accessible from North Glenmore and a straight shot north to Winfield and Oyama.

Recreation & Sports
World-class golf, fitness, and a $251M rec centre

Glenmore has more recreational infrastructure per capita than most Kelowna neighbourhoods — anchored by two golf courses, a rebuilt recreation centre, and a range of private fitness options.

Okanagan Golf Club
Golf · 36 Holes

Two championship courses in Glenmore — the Nicklaus Design Bear Course (par 72) and the Les Furber Quail Course (par 72). One of BC's premier golf destinations. Memberships, public play, and a clubhouse restaurant. Living in Glenmore means this is your local course.

Kelowna Golf & Country Club
Golf · 18 Holes · Est. 1920

One of BC's oldest golf clubs — an 18-hole parkland course established in 1920. Member-focused, mature grounds, and a longstanding Glenmore institution. A short drive from the neighbourhood's established streets.

Parkinson Recreation Centre
Recreation · $251M Rebuild · 2027

Kelowna's landmark recreation centre is undergoing a $251M rebuild and is expected to reopen in 2027. The new facility will include twin ice surfaces, a full aquatics centre, fitness facilities, multi-purpose courts, and community spaces — integrated with the planned Glenmore Secondary School.

The Realm Fitness
Private Gym · Fitness

A well-equipped private gym serving Glenmore — strength training, functional fitness, and group classes. A solid option for residents who want a no-frills training environment close to home.

Oxygen Yoga & Fitness
Yoga & Group Fitness

Hot yoga, yoga, and group fitness classes at a Glenmore-area location. Popular with the neighbourhood's active families, professionals, and active seniors — a staple in the local wellness community.

Geert Maas Sculpture Gardens
Arts & Culture

One of the Okanagan's most unique outdoor art destinations — a working sculptor's studio and gallery set within mature Glenmore gardens. Walking paths wind through large-scale bronze and stone sculptures. Worth a visit for buyers and residents alike.

Faith & Community
A neighbourhood of neighbours

Glenmore is home to a range of faith communities and cultural organizations serving the neighbourhood's diverse residential population.

Glenmore United Church
Christian · United

An established congregation in the heart of Glenmore — welcoming, community-focused, and active in local outreach and programming for all ages.

St. Theresa Catholic Parish
Christian · Catholic

Catholic parish serving the Glenmore community with regular masses, sacraments, youth groups, and faith formation programs for families and individuals.

Evangelical & Baptist Churches
Christian · Various

Several evangelical and Baptist congregations operate in and near Glenmore, offering a range of worship styles and community programming for residents of all backgrounds.

Glenmore Neighbourhood Association
Community Organization

Active neighbourhood association facilitating community events, local advocacy, and resident engagement across the Glenmore community. A strong voice in the city's development planning process.

Wilden Community Events
North Glenmore · Community

The Wilden master-planned community in North Glenmore hosts regular resident events, seasonal gatherings, and community programming — building an active social culture from the ground up in a relatively new development.

Cultural & Community Societies
Cultural Organizations

Various cultural associations and service clubs hold events and community gatherings in and around Glenmore throughout the year, reflecting the neighbourhood's diverse and growing residential base.

Health & Medical
Clinics, dentists, and specialists

Glenmore has a well-established medical and dental presence — named clinics, multiple pharmacies, and Kelowna General Hospital 10 minutes away.

Glenvalley Medical Centre
Primary Care · Family Medicine

Family medicine clinic serving Glenmore residents — GP and family physician services, referrals, and preventive care. One of the established primary care options within the neighbourhood.

Hillside Medical Clinic
Walk-In & Family Medicine

Walk-in and family medicine services accessible from Glenmore. No appointment required for many services — practical for residents who need timely access to non-emergency care and prescriptions.

Kelowna General Hospital
Hospital · ~10 min

Kelowna's full-service hospital is approximately 10 minutes from Glenmore — emergency services, surgical care, specialist clinics, and intensive care all on site.

Glenmore Dental & Glenvalley Dental
Dentistry

Two established dental clinics within the neighbourhood — general dentistry, orthodontics, cosmetic services, and hygiene appointments. Most residents don't need to leave Glenmore for routine dental care.

Dynacare Laboratory
Medical Lab · Blood Work

Community laboratory services for blood work, pathology, and diagnostic testing. Convenient for Glenmore residents managing ongoing health conditions or following up on GP referrals.

Physiotherapy & Allied Health
Allied Health

Physiotherapy, chiropractic, massage therapy, and optometry clinics are scattered throughout Glenmore — convenient for athletes, active families, tradespeople, and seniors managing ongoing care needs.

Getting Around
Well connected in every direction

Glenmore sits at Kelowna's geographic centre — served by transit, close to the airport, and within 15 minutes of most destinations in the city. The Burtch Road Extension will add a new Highway 97 connection by 2027.

BC Transit — Routes 18 & 19
Public Transit · Glenmore

Route 18 (Glenmore–Downtown) and Route 19 (Glenmore–Orchard Park) provide direct transit connections from Glenmore to downtown Kelowna and the city's main shopping centre. Frequent service during peak hours.

BC Transit — Route 23
Public Transit · UBCO & Lake Country

Route 23 connects Glenmore north to UBCO and Lake Country — useful for students, faculty, and North Glenmore residents commuting to the university or heading north. Runs via John Hindle Drive.

Glenmore Road
Main Arterial Road

The spine of the neighbourhood — Glenmore Road runs north–south connecting the established southern streets to North Glenmore, Wilden, and the broader Okanagan. The primary driving corridor for daily errands and commuting.

John Hindle Drive
Connector · UBCO & Airport

Opened in 2018, John Hindle Drive provides a fast east–west connection from North Glenmore to UBCO and Kelowna International Airport — both approximately 10–15 minutes away. A significant quality-of-life improvement for North Glenmore residents.

Burtch Road Extension
New Road · Opening 2027

A $20M+ road extension connecting Burtch Road through to Highway 97 — expected to open by 2027. Will dramatically improve access from central Glenmore to the highway network, reducing pressure on Harvey Avenue and improving commute times.

Kelowna International Airport (YLW)
Airport · ~10–15 min

One of Canada's fastest-growing airports — direct flights to Vancouver, Calgary, Toronto, Seattle, and seasonal sun destinations. A 10–15 minute drive from Glenmore via John Hindle Drive or Highway 97.
