North Glenmore

Kelowna's fastest-growing residential area — master-planned communities, valley views, UBCO access, and the biggest development pipeline in the city.

About North Glenmore

Where Kelowna is growing

North Glenmore is Kelowna's northern growth frontier. Active orchards and farmland give way to master-planned communities — most notably Wilden, BC's largest master-planned community with 1,800+ homes built and Phase 3 planning underway for another ~1,000 more. UBCO is 10 minutes away via John Hindle Drive, Kelowna International Airport is 15 minutes, and the $251M Parkinson Recreation Centre rebuild is targeted to open in 2027. The North Glenmore Sector Study (2026) identifies 4,000 acres for future growth — making this Kelowna's most significant long-term value corridor.

🏘️
Wilden Community

BC's largest master-planned community — 1,800+ homes completed across custom-built estates, semi-detached ranchers, and townhomes. Active orchards and 227 acres of protected natural areas woven throughout. Phase 3 adds approximately 1,000 more homes.

🎓
UBCO at Your Doorstep

UBC Okanagan is 10 minutes away via John Hindle Drive (opened 2018). Kelowna International Airport is a similar drive. Consistent rental demand from students, faculty, and travelling professionals makes North Glenmore a natural investment corridor.

💰
Long-Term Growth Story

The North Glenmore Sector Study identifies 4,000 acres for future development. Population could double to 35,000+. With the Burtch Road Extension connecting to Highway 97 by 2027 and a new secondary school in the OCP, the infrastructure is being built ahead of the demand.
